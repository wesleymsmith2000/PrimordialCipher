Gameplay Documentation

A player-facing help center for the online MVP and a core rules reference for playing by hand with tiles, stickers, magnets, markers, and dice.

Quick Concept

Players build prime-factor tiles called Unus from two hidden factor groups called Duae. Tiles must stay connected to their Anchor through orthogonal adjacency and shared factors. Players draw primes, construct tiles, place them, attack through shared factors, and discover stronger factors through Retromorphosis.

The heart of the game is factor topology: where a tile is placed matters only if its factors can keep the network alive.

Core Components

  • Prime: A factor token. Standard primes are 2, 3, 5, and 7.
  • Duae: One of two factor groups on a tile. Each tile has Duae A and Duae B.
  • Unus: The public tile value. It is the LCM of the two Duae products.
  • Anchor: A 3 by 3 home structure containing edge primes, a central lattice, locks, and core resonance.
  • Resonance: Action currency used to draw, build, place, recover, and later support combat.
  • Counter tokens: Temporary state markers that can block Retromorphosis.
  • Unlock tokens: Mark access to discovered super-primes such as 11, 13, and 17.

Tile Construction

  1. Choose one or more retained primes for Duae A.
  2. Choose one or more retained primes for Duae B.
  3. Multiply the factors in each Duae to get two products.
  4. Set the tile's Unus to the least common multiple of those two products.
  5. Pay construction resonance equal to the number of prime factors in the Unus.
  6. When the Build Spot project is complete, spend placement resonance and put it on a legal board cell.
Example:
Duae A = 2 x 3 = 6
Duae B = 3 x 5 = 15
Unus = LCM(6, 15) = 30
Construction cost = factor count of 30 = 3

Network Rules

  • Tiles connect orthogonally, not diagonally.
  • Allied tiles connect only when at least one Duae on each tile shares a factor.
  • A tile is legal if it touches its own Anchor or connects to an allied tile network.
  • After placement, capture, destruction, or Retromorphosis, run a network check.
  • Any tile that loses its connected path to its Anchor is discarded.
  • Network checks repeat until no more disconnected tiles remain.

Combat Rules

  1. An attack requires orthogonal adjacency and shared Unus factors.
  2. Touching Duae are revealed for the fight.
  3. If the revealed/touching factors share no factor, the attack fails and the attacker is discarded.
  4. Harmony is the count of attacking factors that appear among defending factors. Duplicates count.
  5. Dissonance is the count of defending factors absent from attacking factors. Duplicates count.
  6. The attacker rolls one die per Harmony to produce Melody.
  7. The defender rolls one die per Dissonance to produce Fugue.
  8. Melody greater than Fugue captures the defender. Melody less than Fugue discards the attacker. Equal scores draw.

1Minus one success.
2-3No effect.
4Plus one success.
5-6Plus one success and reroll that die.

Retromorphosis

Retromorphosis transforms an exposed, stable tile into a new form and can discover super-primes. In the MVP, a tile must have at least one revealed Duae and no active counter tokens.

  1. Choose a revealed eligible tile.
  2. Choose one Duae and remove one factor from it.
  3. Add that factor to the other Duae and to the tile's Unus calculation.
  4. Compute the new Unus by averaging the modified Duae products and modified Unus, then round normally.
  5. Factor the result and construct legal replacement Duae.
  6. Place a Retromorphosis counter and add newly discovered factors to the player's factor pool.
  7. If a discovered factor is greater than 7, gain an unlock token for that super-prime.

By-Hand Play Kit

Physical Pieces

Grid board, two 3 by 3 anchors, factor stickers or tokens, tile bases, markers, counters, and six-sided dice.

Recommended Tokens

Use visible Unus labels, concealed Duae factor labels, resonance markers, lock markers, and counter markers.

Hidden Information

Keep Duae concealed until combat, inspection, or rules explicitly reveal them. The Unus remains public.

Manual Turn Procedure

  1. Start your turn and collect the current resonance gain.
  2. Choose actions such as draw, build, pay build progress, place, attack, recover, or Retromorphose.
  3. Update counters, tile ownership, discard stacks, and event notes as each action resolves.
  4. Run network checks after board-changing actions.
  5. End your turn and pass to the next player.
